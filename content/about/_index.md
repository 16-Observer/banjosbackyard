Hi, I'm Austin — the person behind Banjo's Backyard.

I moved into this place to give my two dogs, Banjo and Wookie, more room to run around. In the process, I've welcomed countless chickens, 6 geese, and so much more life into my life. Sadly, Banjo passed away in July of 2024. But my love for him and for dogs has never changed. Thus, Banjo's Backyard was born.

I am on nine acres outside Monroe, Washington, where I spend most of my time building something I'm genuinely proud of: a working homestead with a food forest, a flock of 40+ chickens and geese, and a dog boarding operation that I started because I believe animals deserve more than a kennel. It's a learning experience, but each year I get better, produce more, and develop systems to manage the plants and animals better.

I have over 30 fruit trees planted across my 9 acres — and more are added frequently — along with another 100 varieties of food-producing plants. When I decide to add something to the homestead, I want to make sure it's both unique and easy to grow. More importantly, it needs to be safe for animals to eat.

My driving principle for dog sitting was born out of a simple idea: your dog should come home having had a real experience, not just survived one. Dogs here get open space, real supervision, and a host who actually cares how their stay goes. I keep my books small on purpose so I can keep the quality high. I keep my prices low because your pet is family, and should get treated as such without doubling the cost of your vacation.

When I'm not outside, I'm probably seed starting under grow lights, planning the next stretch of fence, or training for a trail run. Monroe is home, and this land is the project.

Thanks for trusting me with your dog! Let me know if there's a tree you've always wanted planted but don't have the space — I'll see what I can do. And don't hesitate to reach out to share notes and swap stories about homesteading, gardening, chickens, or whatever you fancy.

— Austin
